Erisa Bekteshi

Share

https://durresbiennale.org/en/artists/Albania/artists/Erisa-Bekteshi

Erisa BEKTESHI was born in Durrës. In 2006, he graduated in sculpture at the Academy of Fine Arts in Tirana. After her university studies, she conducted several trainings and seminars in the field of visual art, as well as the author of a series of solo exhibitions at the Jozef Art Gallery, Durrës (2008); Expo art gallery. GR Tirana (2012); SIGAL art club, Tirana (2012); Kalo Gallery, Tirana (2016); Casual VISUA ART, Durrës and Prizren (2018) and participating in joint exhibitions inside and outside Albania at the National Historical Museum; Academy of Fine Arts, Tirana; Gallery Zero etc.


Me or you?
Wrought iron, stainless steel sheet, three tubes in red, blue and yellow, 2 m, 2024


  • The conceptual approach:


    Robotic technologies can be used to depict and display emotions in sculptures, but still have challenges in human beings, in the way they convey and interpret feelings. People often form deeper connections with art and sculpture when they understand that they are dealing with creations brought about by the hand of a human artist, because they more easily identify with the process and emotions that have been invested in their creation. However, robotic technologies can represent an additional form of artistic expression and feeling, especially in terms of the technique and design used to create the sculptures. For example, a sculpture created with robotic technology can be more accurate and detailed in physical form, influencing the perception of viewers.

    Artificial intelligence and art are two fields that represent a combination of ancient and new. For painters and sculptors, the impact of artificial intelligence can be significant. An emerging application is in the search for inspiration and the adaptation of techniques in the creation of art. Using artificial intelligence to analyze artistic trends and public preferences can help artists adapt and create more appropriate works. However, some concerns are related to the replacement of human creativity and the expression of artistic individuality by technology. The presence of artificial intelligence may bring a challenge to the identity and role of the artist in the future hybrid society.
