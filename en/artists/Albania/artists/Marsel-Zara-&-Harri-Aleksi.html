Marsel Zara


Marsel ZARA was born in 1987, in Elbasan. In 2010, he graduated in sculpture-ceramics at the Academy of Fine Arts in Tirana. After university studies, Marcel started working as a graphic designer for Agna Group, where he still works today. From 2022, he holds the position of Artistic Director for the Digital Media department. Marcel Zara in his experience with art, has had several collaborations as an external lecturer at the Faculty of Fine Arts, organizing workshops for animated graphics and as a teacher, part of the Decode Packaging project for the process of creating a package , held at the Graphics Atelier. Between 2010 - 2014, Marsel has had a central role as a graphic designer at Agna Group, developing an exceptional experience with international brands such as: Pepsi Co, Heineken, Amstel, Johnie Walker, Smirnoff, Nestle etc. Currently he develops his creative activity between graphic design at Agna Group, as Artistic Director and pedagogy at the University of Arts in Tirana.


Harri Aleksi


Harri ALEKSI was born in 1981 in Tirana. He is a visual artist and graphic designer. The mediums of his artworks vary from video/animation to interactive installation. He graduated in graphics at the Academy of Fine Arts in Tirana. Harri Aleksi is the author of a number of solo exhibitions and a participant in many joint exhibitions, where the Onufri Re-made competition, Pristina (2020); ME-WE Contemporary Art Center, Kor√ßa (2019); Festival of Contemporary Art and Process Research, Tirana, (2018); the Pamur competition, part of the Book and Contemporary Art Festival; National Historical Museum (2014); Case D exhibition of contemporary art and design (2012); My Odyssey Exhibition, Tirana (2011) and To Tell A Story AMC Prince Exhibition, Tirana (2007). Harri Aleksi has an experience of 18 years of expertise in brand identity, digital printing and packaging design. He is currently a teacher of graphics, digital painting and graphic design at the University of Arts in Tirana.


The Tunnel
Kinetic installation, 400 x 600 cm, 2024


  • The conceptual approach:


    The concept of modern slavery and the value of life in the Information Age is brought through a tunnel that absorbs the audience in a vortex of experience that is both terrifying and reflective. It is the channeling tunnel of commercial, financial, entertainment and even individual life data that comes in the form of a meat-grinder of corporations, political parties, governments, religious organizations, banks, courts and any other form that it awakens their interest to turn this individual from a consumer first, into the very flesh that feeds this unstoppable machine. The site-specific installation that takes place in an ancient arena parallels two different and distant times, but deeply similar in concept. The ancient spectacle that fed on the flesh and blood of the gladiators is today the allegory of the greed of the blade and the propeller that consumes human lives in this vortex of power struggles and sick ideologies, which are catalyzed in the name of human freedom, but have nothing to do with shared with human well-being. Even today they are the cause of the same cyclical atrocities that have always existed.
