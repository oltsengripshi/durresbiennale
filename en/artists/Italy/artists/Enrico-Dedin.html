Enrico Dedin

Share

https://durresbiennale.org/en/artists/Italy/artists/Enrico-Dedin

Enrico DEDIN was born in 1996 in Treviso, Italy. He is an artist and digital creator. In 2018 Dedin graduated in new art technologies at the Academy of Fine Arts in Venice. He has participated in numerous digital art exhibitions, competitions and festivals around the world and cultural events promoted by international entities such as: FAO (The Food and Agriculture Organization) NYC Parks, the New York Mayor's Office of Media and Entertainment, Italian Ministry of Culture, SIAE (Societa Italiana degli Autori ed Editori), Bevilacqua La Masa Art Foundation; Italy; The Wrong Biennale, Spain and EDAF (European Digital Art Fair). He is the winner of several national and international awards such as: Insegne: parole e immagini d'Italia for Contemporary Photography. His works are part of the international video art collection Heure Exquisite.


F.A.Q. Frequently asked questions about art
Video-art, 7:50 min, 2024


  • The conceptual approach:


    In an imaginary one-way dialogue, the artist asks an Artificial Intelligence chatbot 118 rhetorical questions addressed to the contemporary art system. There are 118, like the Italian Pronto Soccorso number, open questions, strictly unanswered, selected from the linguistic pattern, questions from the bottom up, almost to an oracle, to an enigmatic intangible entity. The questions that arise from the lack of opening a dialogue, towards an art system that very often builds invisible walls of the unsaid, indifference and marginalization. For this reason, already in the title "FAQ" we play with homophony with the word "Fuck". The questions therefore focus on existential, sociological, economic, work, philosophical and historical aspects, because they are related to success, fame, the art market and the daily difficulties faced by young artists.
