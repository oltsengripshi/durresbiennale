Fabrizio Passarella

Share

https://durresbiennale.org/en/artists/Italy/artists/Fabrizio-Passarella

Fabrizio Passarella (Azuz Baba) was born in 1952 in Contarina, Italy. He studied at the School of Arts and Painting at the Academy of Fine Arts of Bologna. In the 80s, besides painting, he began to explore other artistic techniques such as video, digital painting and electronic music, increasingly integrating painting, performances, music and primitive electronic videos into his artworks. Passarella has exhibited in many well-known galleries and museums such as: the Gallery of Modern Art in Bologna; Forum Ludwig – Aachen; Kunstmuseum, Bonn; Wilhelm Lehmbruck, Duisburg; Kunstmuseum Düsseldorf; Austellungshalle am Hawerkamp, Münste; Gallery of Modern and Contemporary Art, Bergamo; Museum of Modern Art, Bolzano and Rivara Museum (Fondazione Paludetto).


The desert knows me well
Video-art, 44:12 min, 2019-2022


  • The conceptual approach:


    The content of the video art is divided into three movements, like a piece of music, each dedicated to the main alchemical stages, which unfolds as a kind of fairy-tale pilgrimage through the world of the Middle East (both in sound and in images) a visual and sensory narrative that it starts from the Balkans, continues to Anatolia, to the Caucasus, to the lost Nabataean cities, to the sands of Arabia and to the Persian gardens, arriving at the pearl domes of the Mughals. All this resembles the travels of the artist's youth, when mass tourism and globalization had not yet "devoured" the exoticism of these territories. The video art evidences above all an inner journey of the artist, starting from cruel exile to the lost pleasures of the garden of Love and Truth. A symbolic journey through the desert of life stripped of the dimension of love, which, once you've touched the bottom, can mark a new beginning.
