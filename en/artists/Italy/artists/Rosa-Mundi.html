Rosa Mundi

Share

https://durresbiennale.org/en/artists/Italy/artists/Rosa-Mundi

Rosa MUNDI is the pseudonym of the Italian artist. She was born in 1970 in Venice, Italy. Mundi lives between two sea islands and a river island. She graduated in painting, sculpture, choreography and art history at the Academy of Fine Arts in Venice, the Academie des Beaux Arts in Strasbourg and the Ecole Martenot in Paris. A tracer of past and present civilizations, Rosa Mundi depicts in her art real events from everyday life, superimposing human feelings on them. In her creativity, Mundi has always preferred to use recycled materials, with the aim of giving them a new life, as part of a metamorphosis process. Her art is characterized by a study of light and reflections.


HOMO SINE HOMO: metamorfico postumano
Installation, mixed media, 86 x 30 cm, 2022 – 2024


  • The conceptual approach:


    The works included in the whole installation tell about a world in which man, thanks to his comfort as a being, has given a lot of space to artificial intelligence, which is slowly becoming a necessity to the extent that in the end, the human being risks being replaced by it.
    The two suitcases HOMO DIGITAL WEB and HOMO SINE HOMO metaphorically represent, the first: "The HOMO DIGITAL WEB", the synthesis of the man who renounces his signature (signature), which has traced his identity for centuries indeed, to already delegate it to one of his many digital traces, which leads the human being to the implicit acceptance of a multiplicity of his identities on the web. While the second suitcase, "HOMO SINE HOMO", is the subsequent metamorphosis of man, who thanks to artificial intelligence, almost without realizing it, is replaced by a kind of robot that has memorized, in a large suitcase on wheels, his entire history . On the front and back of the giant transparent suitcase, a kind of humanoid caricature is painted - represented by the silhouette of a running rabbit, patterned with bolts. The rabbit, as a symbol of preserving the immaterial memory of a person, of a person who is already mentally lazy. Another element that completes the installation is the Pinocchio covered with electronic wires (under construction) to remind us that part of human historical memory is also the ability to "lie". Therefore, as a symbol of the "lie", Rosa Mundi chooses the character of Pinocchio, asking him a question: in addition to knowledge, will the artificial intelligence also take over the lie, good or bad?
