Mu Tuan

Share

https://durresbiennale.org/en/artists/Special/artists/Mu-Tuan

Mu TUAN was born in 1994 in Hsinchu. He is an independent visual artist and curator based in Taipei. In 2017, he graduated in painting from the National University of Arts in Taipei, and after his university studies, thanks to advanced technology, he began to look for new ways of reinterpreting the worldview of traditional Asian culture. This using a poetic as well as a philosophical approach to human perception and thought in the age of technology. His works are characterized by East Asian Buddhist and Taoist philosophy and mythological stories. His art combines artificial intelligence, virtual reality, animation, voice performance, painting and calligraphy. Mu Tuan's works constitute an interdisciplinary narrative structure and draw the viewer into a dialogue with the artwork. He is a participant in numerous international exhibitions at the Athens Digital Arts Festival; CutOut Fest; Miami New Media Festival; Now & After International Video Art Festival; Venice Immersive Market Taiwan Pavilion and ECRA Festival.


Calligrapedia: A Universal Algorithm #2 / Calligrapedia: A Universal Algorithm #2
Single-channel, Generative Adversarial Network, Color, Stereo, 2’32”, 2024


  • The conceptual approach:


    Based on the artist's experience in practicing A Narrative of Calligraphy, for many years, Calligraphedia: A Universal Algorithm, uses a machine learning model called Generative Adversarial Network to analyze a large number of calligraphy drafts. Drawing inspiration from natural elements such as landscapes, animals and plants, he developed a series of man-made and machine-made Chinese characters that flow between writing and organic things. On the other hand, through collaboration with a folk storyteller, he reinterprets ancient oral stories, passed down around the world with "qualitative transformation" as the essence. Breaking down the machine learning aspects of image style recognition and simulation, Calligraphedia: A Universal Algorithm explores how words, language, and mythological stories in human history continually interpret themselves through learning from the world around them. The work constructs an interwoven vision of human-machine-nature interaction.
