Joaquin Fargas

Share

https://durresbiennale.org/en/artists/Argentina/artists/Joaquin-Fargas

Joaquín FARGAS was born in 1950 in Buenos Aires, Argentina. He is an artist, founder and director of the Exploratorio Arts, Science and Technology Center in Buenos Aires and director of the LatBio Lab (Latin American BioArt Laboratory). In 1975 he graduated in industrial engineering at the University of Buenos Aires. In 1992 he graduated in arts and holography at New York University, USA. He has participated in important contemporary art exhibitions such as: Havana Biennial (2016); Galileo Galilei Planetarium, Buenos Aires (2015); Centro Cultural Recoleta, Buenos Aires (2013); and San Martin Theater, Buenos Aires (2012). Fargas integrates art, science and technology to create his works. His art focuses on possible or utopian propositions related to life, its preservation and interconnection between living beings and the future. His art integrates biological and technological materials aimed at generating possible dialogues and hybrid ecologies. Since 2000, his artistic creativity is based on robotics, crypto art, quantum computing and artificial intelligence. He currently works and lives in Buenos Aires.


GAIA 3.0
Video-art, 12:08 min, 2022


  • The conceptual approach:


    From Gaia to Pachamama. In Greek mythology, Gaia is the personification of our planet. Likewise, the Incas had their own version of Mother Earth called Pachamama. Gaia 3.0 begins a dialogue between technology and nature, emphasizing that the future of humanity will depend on our actions today. The Earth, our planet, is approximately 4.5 billion years old, a space unimaginable to our minds, far exceeding the scale by which we measure our personal time. Even the existence of humans as a species on Earth is negligible in the vastness of the Universe. A better understanding of our planet can help us reconnect with nature, recognizing both its strength and its vulnerability. As we have evolved, we have distanced ourselves from nature, often forgetting our fundamental role within it. We must embrace our interconnectedness and work actively to protect and educate our environment. This renewed relationship with nature is essential to establishing the harmony we so desperately need. The word Quechua Pacha has multiple meanings: time, era, universe, earth, world, cosmos, space, nature, hour, place, but the most important are: time-space and Universe, because Pacha means the manifestation of existence, the world of the visible and the the invisible.
