Maria Bordeanu

Share

https://durresbiennale.org/en/artists/Romania/artists/Maria-Bordeanu

Maria BORDEANU was born in 1982 in Bucharest, Romania. In 2007, she graduated in painting at the Department of Fine Arts at the University of Arts in Bucharest. Her works combine intricate, detail-oriented imagery inspired by nature, photography and elements of baroque art. Since 2011, the art of artist Bordeanu is represented by Gallery 418 (Cetate/Munich). The artistic approach of the Romanian artist revolves around photorealistic, figurative and cinematic themes subtly filled with elements of media culture, alongside dystopian elements and post-human visual metaphors. Among the recent projects are a series of works on the subject of reflections and mirrors. In 2023 she was part of the Arte Laguna finalist exhibition in Venice. She currently lives and works in Stockholm, Sweden.


Mirrors
Installation, oil on canvas, (9) 40 x 40 cm secila, 2021-2022


  • The conceptual approach:


    The series of mirrors evidences a strong liking of the Romanian artist for realistic images ranging from film stills, to artificial landscapes to the interiors of various buildings. Mirrors as a reflection on the multitude of images that surround us, occupy a large part in my artistic creativity. Such an approach helps the artist to explore more types of images in developing the mirror series, including text, close-ups and figure-like images. This way, which combines the real truth with the artificial truth on the canvas. The series of mirrors are on round canvases and when placed together, create compositions filtered through a personal perspective. Blue and silver colors are used to reflect images of reality and sculptural forms.
    The painting "Interrupted Video" was inspired by the message on Youtube and I liked how it resonates with us: can we keep track of the things around us? Are we so connected to technology, or can we stop and rethink what to do next?
