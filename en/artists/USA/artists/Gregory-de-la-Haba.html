Gregory de la Haba

Share

https://durresbiennale.org/en/artists/USA/artists/Gregory-de-la-Haba

Gregory De La HABA was born in 1979 in Astoria, New York in the United States of America. He is an artist, writer, curator and interdisciplinary cultural producer. In 1993 he graduated with honors in liberal arts from Harvard University. Artist De La Haba's art has been exhibited in galleries and art fairs around the world, including: META Gallery; Monte Carlo; East End Collected4; Southampton Arts Centre; Milan Design Week; Elga Wimmer Gallery, New York, Mykonos Biennale (2014), Salzak Biennale, Salzburg Arts Festival; Queens Museum; Kunsthaus Tacheles; Contemporary Istanbul SCOPE Art Show; Art Basel Miami and Art Southampton.

In 2009, De La Haba was Artist-in-Residence at Jack the Pelican Presents. In 2004 McSorley exhibited his Sketchbook series at the Glucksman Ireland House at New York University, accompanied by an essay written by Pulitzer Prize-winning author Frank McCourt. De La Haba is represented by Geuer & Geuer Art GmbH, Dusseldorf, Germany. De La Haba's art is treated philosophically by Bryan Reynolds in his book: The Transversality of Gregory de la Haba: The Future of Art and Myth are on Us. His works have been part of theater performances and films such as: Hazel: Made In Belfast at Carnegie Hall NY (2014); How And Why I Robbed My First Cheese Store (2011); Fishing for Paradise at La MaMa Theater NY (2014), as well as Allan Weisbecker's Water Time: Surf Travel Diary of a Madman (2014).
