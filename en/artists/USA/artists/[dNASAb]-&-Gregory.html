[dNASAb]


[dNASAb] is an American artist. He majored in sculpture and mixed media at Florida State University in the United States of America. Artist [dNASAb] has developed a distinguished career in the field of visual art after his undergraduate studies, holding several distinguished artist residencies at the Experimental Television Center, Oëgo, NY (2006) and the Institute for Electronic Arts, Alfred University, New York (2010).

In 2010 he won a fellowship at Harvestworks Digital Media Arts Center, New York. [dNASAb] is the author of several solo exhibitions at: Volta – International Art Fair, NY (2011); Irvine Contemporary in Washington D.C. (2011); Frederieke Taylor Gallery, NY (2010) and Art Basel Miami (2009), garnering the attention of the Washington Post; Sculpture magazine; Art Papers; ART 21 and Reuters Television. In 2010 he was honored with Brooklyn Art: Work at the BRIC Contemporary Art Gala. [dNASAb] has exhibited his art around the world, collaborating with industry giants such as: Microsoft, Apple and Bloomberg. The artist lives and works in Brooklyn, NY.


Gregory de la Haba


Gregory De La HABA was born in 1979 in Astoria, New York in the United States of America. He is an artist, writer, curator and interdisciplinary cultural producer. In 1993 he graduated with honors in liberal arts from Harvard University. Artist De La Haba's art has been exhibited in galleries and art fairs around the world, including: META Gallery; Monte Carlo; East End Collected4; Southampton Arts Centre; Milan Design Week; Elga Wimmer Gallery, New York, Mykonos Biennale (2014), Salzak Biennale, Salzburg Arts Festival; Queens Museum; Kunsthaus Tacheles; Contemporary Istanbul SCOPE Art Show; Art Basel Miami and Art Southampton.

In 2009, De La Haba was Artist-in-Residence at Jack the Pelican Presents. In 2004 McSorley exhibited his Sketchbook series at the Glucksman Ireland House at New York University, accompanied by an essay written by Pulitzer Prize-winning author Frank McCourt. De La Haba is represented by Geuer & Geuer Art GmbH, Dusseldorf, Germany. De La Haba's art is treated philosophically by Bryan Reynolds in his book: The Transversality of Gregory de la Haba: The Future of Art and Myth are on Us. His works have been part of theater performances and films such as: Hazel: Made In Belfast at Carnegie Hall NY (2014); How And Why I Robbed My First Cheese Store (2011); Fishing for Paradise at La MaMa Theater NY (2014), as well as Allan Weisbecker's Water Time: Surf Travel Diary of a Madman (2014).


Rage Against The Machine Learning: AI, AR, and the Revolt of Reality
Oil on printed canvas and video projection, 4 x 5 m, 2024


  • The conceptual approach:


    By uniting traditional fine art practices with advanced technologies, Gregory De La Haba and [dNASAb] bring to the Durres International Biennale of Contemporary Art a visually stunning, symbiotic dynamic – a true hybridity of art born from the ashes of history. throughout and the history of art with its magic, and the future potential through Artificial Intelligence. With a thematic focus on the energy that connects every living organism in the face of environmental care and technological innovation (especially "Blockchain" and AI), the artists by alienating their sculptures and paintings into immersive experiences, try to engage the public as co-creators and participants in these new, exciting spaces that constantly change shape and volume. Gregory De La Haba and [dNASA], thanks to the power of their art, want to make everyone aware of the rapid technological developments and the direct impact that advanced technology has on the daily lives of each of us.
